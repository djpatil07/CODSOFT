Phishing Awareness

Learn how phishing attacks work and how to stay safe online.

Learn More

Phishing is a cyber attack where scammers trick you into revealing personal information by pretending to be a trusted source.

Types of Phishing

Email Phishing

Attackers send fake emails pretending to be trusted companies to steal information.

SMS Phishing (Smishing)

Scammers send fake text messages with malicious links or urgent warnings.

Fake Websites

Hackers create websites that look real to trick you into entering passwords or banking details.

Social Media Scams

Scammers impersonate friends or brands to steal data or money through social platforms.

Common Signs of Phishing

  • Suspicious or shortened links
  • Unknown or misspelled sender address
  • Unexpected attachments
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Grammar and spelling mistakes
  • Requests for personal information

Phishing Examples

Example 1: Fake Bank Email

From: support@fakebank.com

Message: Your account has been suspended. Click here to reactivate.

Example 2: Social Media Scam

From: facebook-notify@scam.com

Message: You have a new friend request. Login here to see who it is.

How to Stay Safe from Phishing

1️⃣

Check the sender’s email address carefully before clicking any links.

2️⃣

Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking.

3️⃣

Do not download attachments from unknown sources.

4️⃣

Use multi-factor authentication wherever possible.

5️⃣

Report suspicious emails immediately to your IT department or relevant authority.

Report a Phishing Attempt

If you found a suspicious activity, report it immediately to the proper authority.
